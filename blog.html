2.Simple Guide to Healthy Eating

What is Healthy Eating?

Healthy eating isn’t about strict rules, being very thin, or avoiding foods you enjoy. It’s about balance — eating meals that give you energy, improve your health, and make you feel good. The focus is on overall eating patterns, not single foods.

Basics of a Balanced Diet

~Protein: Supports mood and brain function. Include both plant and animal sources.
~Fats: Not all fats are bad. Unsaturated fats (like olive oil, nuts, fish) protect your heart and brain.
~Carbohydrates: Choose whole grains, vegetables, and fruits instead of sugary or refined foods.
~Fiber: Found in fruits, vegetables, beans, and whole grains. Helps digestion and lowers disease risk.
~Calcium: Important for bones and also affects mood and sleep. Get it from dairy, leafy greens, or fortified foods.

Tips for Switching to Healthy Eating

~Make small changes, like adding a salad once a day.
~Cook more meals at home to control ingredients.
~Replace unhealthy fats with healthy ones (e.g., grilled fish instead of fried chicken).
~Read food labels to know what’s really inside.
~Drink plenty of water to stay energized and avoid mistaking thirst for hunger.

Moderation Matters

~Eat until satisfied, not stuffed.
~Don’t ban favorite foods — enjoy them occasionally.
~Watch portion sizes (meat = size of a deck of cards, carbs = size of a light bulb).
~Slow down when eating; it takes time for your body to feel full.
~Snack wisely with balanced options like fruit with yogurt or crackers with cheese.

Eating Habits

~Eat breakfast to start your day with energy.
~Have smaller meals throughout the day to keep energy steady.
~Try not to eat late at night, as it may lead to weight gain.

Add More Fruits and Vegetables

~Aim for at least 5 servings daily.
~Examples: half a cup of fruit/veg, or one small apple/banana.

Easy ways to add more:
*Mix berries into cereal
*Swap rice/pasta for salad
*Snack on carrots or cherry tomatoes with hummus
*Roast or grill veggies with spices for flavor
